Where Consensus Might Be Wrong
Consensus: Agentic AI is the dominant investment theme and every company should be pivoting to an 'agentic' narrative.
Counter: The security infrastructure for deployed agents doesn't exist yet — $500k wallet drains from LLM router exploits suggest we are deploying agents faster than we are securing them. The category may face a high-profile incident that resets enterprise adoption timelines.

Evidence: 26 LLM router compromises documented this week; financial losses already occurring in production environments. No major security framework for agentic AI tooling has emerged.

Consensus: Outcome-based pricing is the natural evolution for AI-native companies and will dominate.
Counter: Outcome attribution in enterprise software is notoriously contested — customers will accept outcome pricing in pilots but negotiate back to SaaS equivalents at renewal when they realize they can't audit the measurement. The model may be a sales tactic, not a durable pricing architecture.

Evidence: Crosby Legal raised on this thesis but is still early-stage; no mature company has demonstrated sustained outcome-based pricing at scale without significant churn or renegotiation.

Consensus: Prediction markets are now legitimate institutional infrastructure after Kalshi's $1B raise.
Counter: Kalshi's capital raise may be heavily correlated with the 2024 US election cycle, which produced anomalous prediction market volume. Without an equivalent political catalyst in 2025-2026, retention and volume metrics may disappoint institutional investors.

Evidence: Both Kalshi transactions appeared the same week with undisclosed terms on one — structured deal dynamics suggest complexity, not a clean institutional endorsement.

Patterns Tracking
◆ Agentic AI Revenue Inflection [building]

Fleet $1M→$63M ARR in 6 months; Hightouch Series C with production deployments at Spotify and DocuSign; Luminai $38M Series B for healthcare workflow automation.

Why: The agentic AI wave is compressing go-to-market timelines because the ROI is measurable in real-time — customers can see deflected costs or increased revenue within weeks, not quarters. This changes how fast sales cycles close.

Counter: Fleet may be an outlier in sales ops — a category with unusually measurable ROI and high tolerance for automation. Most enterprise categories will have 3-5x longer adoption curves due to change management friction.

◆ Agent Infrastructure Security as Attack Surface [building]

26 LLM routers compromised with $500k wallet drained via malicious tool call injection — first high-dollar financial exploit of agentic AI infrastructure documented this week.

Why: As agents gain autonomous action capability (executing payments, managing credentials), they inherit all the attack surface of traditional software plus new LLM-specific vectors. Security is not keeping pace with deployment.

Counter: This may be a narrow class of poorly-architected deployments — companies that properly sandbox tool permissions won't face this. The risk is real but overstated for well-engineered systems.

◆ Outcome-Based AI Pricing Model [building]

Crosby Legal raised $60M explicitly on 'selling the work' thesis — outcome-based pricing rather than SaaS licensing. Multiple AI-native services companies adopting this framing.

Why: AI reduces the marginal cost of delivering professional services toward zero, which destroys SaaS pricing leverage but creates new opportunity: if you can price on outcomes, your margin ceiling is the client's value created, not their software budget.

Counter: Outcome-based pricing requires robust measurement infrastructure and creates revenue volatility — most enterprise buyers will negotiate SaaS equivalents anyway because outcome attribution is too contested.

■ Prediction Markets Legitimization [established]

Kalshi closed $1B growth round this week — the largest prediction markets raise ever — plus a second undisclosed round. Capital intensity signals institutional LP interest, not just retail.

Why: Regulatory clarity post-2024 election cycle, combined with crypto infrastructure maturity, has made prediction markets fundable at institutional scale. This is a category legitimization event, not a single company story.

Counter: Kalshi's raise is heavily linked to the political cycle — prediction markets that rode 2024 election volume may see demand cliff in non-election years. The $1B bet may be timing-dependent.

◆ Specialized Compute for Agentic AI [building]

SiFive raised $400M Series G specifically for data center processors optimized for agentic AI workloads — infrastructure capital is flowing to the constraint layer.

Why: GPU compute was built for training; agentic inference workloads (low-latency, persistent context, tool orchestration) have different hardware requirements. Specialized silicon is being funded because general-purpose GPUs are economically inefficient for deployed agents.

Counter: NVIDIA's roadmap may absorb this demand before SiFive scales — specialized silicon has a brutal time-to-market problem against a company with NVIDIA's manufacturing and software ecosystem advantages.
