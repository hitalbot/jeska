Counter: Architecture standards in AI have a shelf life of 18 months before the next capability jump obsoletes them. Companies building to today's three-layer standard may find themselves optimizing for a paradigm that GPT-6 makes irrelevant.

Counter: AISI cyber range completion is an Anthropic marketing event as much as a genuine security benchmark. Formal evaluations lag real-world threats by definition — passing yesterday's test says nothing about tomorrow's attack vector.
Outcome-Based AI Pricing Model BUILDING
Insufficient new deal or signal data this week to update status. Pattern holds from prior weeks — no contradicting evidence.
Counter: Outcome-based pricing requires trustworthy outcome measurement. In complex workflows, attribution is murky — 'did the AI close the deal or did the human?' is a question most enterprise buyers can't answer cleanly.
Prediction Markets Legitimization ESTABLISHED
Kalshi disclosed an additional undisclosed round. Continued capital activity with no new structural evidence this week — pattern holds from prior weeks.
Counter: Prediction markets attract sophisticated actors who systematically exploit information asymmetry. Retail participation — the engine of liquidity and revenue — may drop sharply once users experience consistent losses against better-informed counterparties.
Specialized Compute for Agentic AI ESTABLISHED
Arm launched production-ready agent-specific silicon this week. Tesla AI5 taped out — chip design locked, manufacturing started, with AI6 and Dojo3 in development. This is production hardware, not roadmap.
Counter: Arm's agent chip is a marketing story built on existing architecture. True agent-specific silicon requires fundamentally different memory hierarchies and instruction sets. The hardware may be 'optimized' in the way airlines optimize boarding — it sounds good but doesn't move the needle.

Counter: Defense tech VC is structurally dependent on government contracts that take years to materialize. Three undisclosed leads and zero named customers across $2.4B of rounds suggests this may be strategic capital concentration without near-term revenue — the acqui-hire risk is real.

Counter: Quality gaps in Google's AI get fixed faster than any startup can capitalize on them. The perception gap is a 6-month window, not a durable moat.
