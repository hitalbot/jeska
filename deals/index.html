Deal Radar — Week of Apr 6–10, 2026

Sources scanned: AlleyWatch (5 editions), Crunchbase Daily (5), Axios Pro Rata (1). TLDR ran but had no deal content. StrictlyVC, Fortune Term Sheet, TechCrunch, CB Insights just subscribed — will start contributing next week.

The Big Picture

Q1 2026 was a record quarter. North American startups raised $252B+ — more than 3x the prior quarter and the largest quarterly total ever. Nearly two-thirds of global VC went to just four companies. AI took the lion's share, but early/seed-stage funding stayed robust across robotics, semiconductors, and AI infrastructure. An unprecedented number of early-stage unicorns were minted in Q1 alone.

Private credit is flashing warning signs — Carlyle's credit fund saw 15.7% redemption requests (3x the cap), and Blue Owl and BlackRock are getting more asks than they can fill. Dan Primack's take: PE rode the private credit wave. It may get hit by breakers.

Portfolio-Relevant Deals

Spade — $40M | Fintech/data infrastructure

Turns messy transaction data into a strategic asset for banks and fintechs. Stripe, PayPal, and Mercury now depend on it as core infrastructure. The problem: payment data hasn't meaningfully changed in 50 years, costing FIs billions in disputes, fraud, and missed AI opportunities.

→ Tapestry, Hopscotch overlap (fintech). Worth watching — Spade is becoming the pipes layer that consumer crypto/payments companies will sit on top of.

Patlytics — $40M | AI/legal tech

AI-driven patent filing analysis and IP litigation intelligence. Already used by 40%+ of Am Law 100 firms. Patent filings AND IP litigation are both surging because of AI — Patlytics benefits from both sides.

→ Vybe, Mainframe overlap (AI). Different application but same underlying thesis: AI tooling for domain experts.

Kalshi — $1B late-stage (led NYC's $3.94B March) | Fintech/prediction markets

NYC-based prediction market platform. This round led a historic March for NYC VC — $3.94B across 97 deals, 293.6% YoY surge. Kalshi alone was $1B of that. NYC captured 20.7% of all US venture capital, with Series A dominance (39.8% of national deals).

→ Tapestry, Hopscotch overlap (fintech). The NYC stat matters — Stellation's NYC thesis continues to have tailwinds.

Juno — $12M Seed | Fintech/AI (San Francisco)

AI-assisted tax prep. Founded by CPA Dave Haase, automates up to 90% of tax-prep grunt work. Already at hundreds of firms and mid-seven-figure ARR. Crunchbase exclusive.

→ Tapestry, Hopscotch (fintech); Vybe, Mainframe (AI). Classic "AI for domain experts" play — same thesis as PromptLayer.

Market Signals

Fintech consolidating up: Funding rose in Q1 despite ~30% fewer deals. Investors are concentrating into "high-conviction" bets. Fewer companies, bigger checks.
NYC having a moment: $3.94B in March alone. 20.7% of all US VC. Series A dominance nationally.
LatAm shift: Mexico topped Brazil as LatAm's biggest VC market for only the second time since 2012.
AI everywhere: Even "non-AI" categories (tax prep, patent law, ferries) are getting funded on AI theses.
Private credit stress → PE risk: Watch this. If private credit contracts, PE dealmaking contracts with it.
